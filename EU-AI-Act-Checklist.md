# EU AI Act Compliance Checklist
## For Israeli Tech Companies Entering the EU Market

> Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 | Enforcement begins: August 2026
> Prepared for: Israeli law firms and in-house legal teams

---

## Step 1: Determine if EU AI Act applies

The EU AI Act applies if your AI system is:
- [ ] Placed on the EU market, OR
- [ ] Put into service in the EU, OR
- [ ] Output is used in the EU (even if company is based outside EU)

**→ If any box is checked: EU AI Act applies to your company.**

Israeli companies selling SaaS to EU customers are covered even if all servers are in Israel.

---

## Step 2: Classify your AI system's risk level

### Unacceptable Risk (PROHIBITED — Article 5)
These are banned outright. Check if your system:
- [ ] Uses subliminal manipulation to harm users
- [ ] Exploits vulnerabilities of specific groups
- [ ] Social scoring by public authorities
- [ ] Real-time remote biometric identification in public spaces (with narrow exceptions)
- [ ] Emotion recognition in workplace or education

**→ If any apply: product cannot be placed on EU market.**

---

### High Risk (Article 6 + Annex III)
Strict obligations apply. Check if your AI is used in:
- [ ] Biometric identification or categorization
- [ ] Critical infrastructure (water, energy, transport)
- [ ] Education (access decisions, grading)
- [ ] Employment (CV screening, hiring, promotion, termination)
- [ ] Essential services (credit scoring, insurance, benefits)
- [ ] Law enforcement
- [ ] Migration and border control
- [ ] Administration of justice

**→ If any apply: see High Risk Obligations below.**

#### High Risk Obligations Checklist
- [ ] Conduct and document a Conformity Assessment
- [ ] Implement a Risk Management System (ongoing, not one-time)
- [ ] Ensure training data is relevant, representative, and free of errors
- [ ] Maintain technical documentation (Article 11)
- [ ] Enable automatic logging of events (Article 12)
- [ ] Ensure human oversight mechanisms (Article 14)
- [ ] Achieve appropriate accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity (Article 15)
- [ ] Register system in EU database before deployment (Article 16)
- [ ] Appoint EU representative if company is outside EU (Article 25)

---

### Limited Risk (Article 50)
Transparency obligations only:
- [ ] If AI interacts with humans → disclose it is an AI
- [ ] If AI generates synthetic content → label it as AI-generated
- [ ] If AI generates deep fakes → mark clearly

---

### Minimal Risk
No specific obligations. Majority of AI applications fall here.

---

## Step 3: General Provider Obligations (all non-minimal risk)

- [ ] Establish quality management system (Article 17)
- [ ] Keep technical documentation for 10 years post-market
- [ ] Report serious incidents to national authority (Article 73)
- [ ] Cooperate with national supervisory authorities

---

## Step 4: Israel-specific considerations

| Issue | Practical implication |
|-------|----------------------|
| No EU establishment | Must appoint EU representative (Article 25) |
| IL-EU data adequacy | Israel has EU adequacy decision — data transfers permitted |
| Hebrew-language systems | Must still comply if output affects EU persons |
| Israeli government clients | Check if use case triggers public authority rules |

---

## Key deadlines

| Date | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| February 2025 | Prohibited practices rules apply |
| August 2025 | GPAI model rules apply |
| August 2026 | Full enforcement for high-risk systems |
| August 2027 | Existing high-risk systems must comply |
